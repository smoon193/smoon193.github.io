Clips

Once I had gotten my basic shapes out of the way, I came back on Sunday ready to design my clips. I got to the lab a bit early so I could be fully prepped and ready to make the most of Nathan's office hours and........ I realized I had forgotten to bring cardboard 💀💀💀💀💀💀 There was none in the recycling bin at the mill, the front desk person said they didn't have any extra, and I checked one more place on campus before resorting to scrounging behind a building in the U District. You know you're down bad when you see this and think JACKPOT!!!! 😂🥴
I was trying to keep it as simple as possible, so I thought about a straight clip with notches on either end to attach sheets side by side, and a sort of corner joint version of this so I could make a box. I designed first clip to be about 4cm x 3cm. Cura estimated it would take over 20 minutes, which I decided was too long to test out the first prototype. I asked Nathan and he agreed that that seemed long, so we tried a shorter version and I also finally downloaded Prusa Slicer to see if that made a difference in speed. With the shorter clip model in Prusa Slicer it said it should be more like 6 minutes, so I tested it out.

This one passed the shake test! But then I was thinking about how long it would take to print all the full-sized ones, and started to wonder what I would even make with them anyways. Every minute that I'm not printing cute little doll things with my translucent hot pink filament feels like I am disobeying the will of Goddess herself, so I thought if I made the clips really small I could make a palatial Barbie dream house... out of little plastic bits and dirty cardboard... ok maybe it's more like Barbie's starter home. I created a smaller version and tried printing 10 more after the first test came out well. Since this clip wasn't so tall, I tried it without a brim, hoping this would be nice for multiples since it was tough to get the brim out of the notch of the first test.

No brim = DISASTER!!! My filament also got horrendously tangled somehow. But even after untangling it, the second round of clips weren't sticking well to the bed. I gave up on the brimless idea and printed 14 more with a brim!

I was still really hoping to make corner joints so that I could make the four walls the house and a roof. I was really stuck in Rhino because somehow I must have trimmed a couple segments of the lines that were right on the axes so I couldn't see that they were missing. Once I realized this and fixed it I was able to extrude the corner joint shape and print it.
