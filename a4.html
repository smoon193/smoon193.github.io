Option 2: Design and 3D print a box that press-fits together tightly enough to hold sugar.



I read through the slides and watched a few woodworking videos about different types of joins and decided to just wing it and try out a couple tiny panels with box joints to get a baseline to see what I'd need to adjust.

Attempt 1

For my first attempt, I wanted to see what would happen if I just asked the printer to make panels with the same size of fingers as grooves (4mm each). I was curious how close the flexibility of the material and the error of the printer would get me to a working box joint. The joints would not fit together at all, but they seemed pretty close. I measured each of the fingers to see how accurate the print was. For the piece with 3 fingers, I measured each of them at about 4.06mm. I remembered the slides had some suggestions for what to start adjusting if your print was precise but not accurate, but then when I measured the piece with two fingers, I got measurements between 3.98mm and 4.06mm, which seemed like a pretty wide range relative to the difference one of the woodworking videos said there should be between groove and finger. This also threw off any potential adjustments I was thinking of making since it was no longer simply a problem of the whole print being off by the same amount consistently.
